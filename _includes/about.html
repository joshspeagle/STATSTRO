About the Workshop

STATSTRO (formerly Stellar Stats) is an annual interdisciplinary workshop bringing together researchers from astrophysics, statistics, computer science, and related fields to celebrate and foster interdisciplinary research at the University of Toronto and beyond.

Now in its seventh year, the workshop aims to help foster new connections between statistics and astronomy, to provide a fun venue to highlight research opportunities, and to help early-career researchers — especially students and postdocs — develop networks outside their home departments.

This year's theme, {{ site.conference.tagline }}, explores how modern sampling methods, computational simulations, and statistical inference are driving new discoveries across the sciences. The workshop features four thematic sessions, each with a headline overview talk, a hands-on coding tutorial, lightning talks, and an invited science presentation.
